Bay Adapt is a geospatial analysis of the San Francisco Bay shoreline to inform sea level rise adaptation strategy decisions at the regional and local level. This interactive mapping tool can be used by planners in coastal communities throughout the Bay Area to assess what physical adaptation strategies are most likely to be applicable to their respective shorelines. The map divides the Bay Area coastline into 100-ft segments and classifies each based on edge condition, slope, and exposure to wave action.

This interactive mapping tool can be used by planners in coastal communities throughout the Bay Area to assess what physical adaptation strategies are most likely to be applicable to their respective shorelines. Bay Adapt is intended to inform conversations about which strategies to study further at the site level.
